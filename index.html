Economic Sanctions & Strategic Risk

Beyond list-screening and technical compliance, sanctions function as instruments of economic statecraft with cascading second-order effects on institutional behavior, market structure, and strategic optionality. Modern sanctions regimes operate through financial connectivity rather than territorial jurisdiction, creating exposure that traditional risk frameworks struggle to capture.

Current focus: Secondary sanctions architecture, extraterritoriality as a design feature, shadow fleet enforcement mechanisms, and the evolution of sanctions from compliance checkboxes to systemic risk frameworks.

AML/CFT & Financial Crime Prevention

The global shift toward beneficial ownership transparency has fundamentally altered the compliance landscape. With the implementation of the Corporate Transparency Act and the 6th AML Directive, institutions must now conduct structural analysis of multi-layered ownership to satisfy enhanced due diligence requirements that extend far beyond traditional name-screening.

Current focus: Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO) verification methodologies, Source of Wealth investigations in complex trust structures, AI-driven transaction monitoring, and the intersection of compliance obligations with strategic risk management.

White Collar Enforcement & Corporate Investigations

White collar enforcement has evolved beyond traditional securities fraud to encompass trade-based financial crime, customs fraud, and anti-bribery compliance. The DOJ's refined approach to FCPA enforcement—prioritizing cases where foreign bribery directly harms competitive fairness—reflects a strategic shift toward protecting economic interests alongside legal compliance.

Current focus: FCPA and anti-bribery & corruption (ABC) enforcement trends, trade and customs fraud as national security threats, third-party due diligence frameworks, and emerging patterns in corporate fraud detection including "AI washing" investigations.
